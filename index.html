Philosophy

Summary

Overall, I strongly believe in the constructivist learning philosophy. Constructivism includes a vairety of learning theories coined by renowened philosphers such as John Dewey, Jean Piaget and Lev Vygotsky. Additionally I think it is importance to enable students to reach higher order thinking by scaffolding questions using Allan Bloom's taxonomy. I have implmented constructivist learning actvities throughout my lessons while teaching, you can view them here: lesson plans .

Constructivism

Constuctivism is based on the idea that people actively construct or make their own knowledge and that reality is determined by your experince as a learner. In simplified terms, learners use their prevsious knowledge as a foundation and build on it with new things that they learn. Project or inquiry-based actvties are types of activties that are constructivist based. Constructivism facilitates critical thinking, analysis, problem-solving and creativity.

Bloom's Taxonomy

Bloom's Taxonomy is a hierachy of different skills levels that can be catergorised as either lower or higher order thinking skills. It provides examples of question verbs to stimulate the development of each of the skills. Higher order thinking skills are important as they are life-long skills that a leaner can use in a variety of contexts throughout their life.
